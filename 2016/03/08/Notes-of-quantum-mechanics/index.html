Prologue

Recently (a week ago?) I finished learning quantum mechanics, for the first time. Although there are many topics I haven’t understood, by next semester, I will not learn it again.

Bibliography

Griffith, Sakurai, Shankar

Actually, there is no perfect textbook of quantum mechanics. Relatively, Sakurai is the best one, but it assumes that you have learnt classical mechanics (Goldstein) and elementary quantum mechanics (a.k.a. wave mechanics). Griffith and Shankar are both self-contained, but they are not very interesting.

In addition, all of them treat approximation methods (i.e. WKB, variational methods and perturbation) and scattering theory terribly. I think they will be explained better in field theory (so know I am trying to learn relativistic quantum mechanics and scattering theory from Weinberg’s QFT).

I want to register the course “advanced quantum mechanics” next semester and revisit quantum mechanics. I plan to follow

Landau, Quantum mechanics (non-relativistic theory).
Weinberg, Lectures on quantum mechanics.
Hall, quantum theory for mathematicians.
